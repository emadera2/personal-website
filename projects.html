Project 1: LatinXTech Non-Profit

The purpose of this project was to combat the digital divide, particularly the one affecting the Hispanic community. My peers and I decided to take action on this issue because Hispanic students make up only 7% of Computer Science majors and only 18% of the STEM field. We started by identifying the key barriers that Hispanic students face in pursuing STEM education and careers. These barriers included lack of access to technology, limited exposure to STEM subjects, financial constraints, and a shortage of role models and mentors in these fields. Our goal was to create a comprehensive program that addresses these challenges and empowers Hispanic students to pursue STEM careers.

Project 2: Personal Website

This can be particularly advantageous when applying for jobs, internships, or academic opportunities, as it provides a comprehensive overview of my qualifications beyond what a traditional resume can convey. Additionally, the process of building and maintaining a website allows me to hone my technical skills, such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Ultimately, my personal website is a dynamic and evolving platform that represents my personal and professional growth, creativity, and dedication to my career goals.
